# Polymarket Wallet-Discovery Sweep — 2026-06-10

**Goal:** find NEW early-entry TAKER wallets with VERIFIED realized edge that are mechanically copyable.
**Context:** the prior copy-trading cohort was forward-falsified (polymarket-2dollar-bot 21-daemon walk), and the
#1 all-time wallet `0x55be7aa0…` (coinman2) was reclassified as a maker/merge bot (see `docs/POLYMARKET-STACK-AUDIT.md`) —
makers cannot be copied, their fills ARE the edge. Stance: `TRADING_POLICY.md` — pro-trading, anti-delusion.

## Method

1. `npm run scan:leaderboard` — DAY/WEEK/MONTH × top-50 PnL leaderboards (`data-api.polymarket.com/v1/leaderboard`),
   sustained filter: appears in ≥2 windows, PnL ≥ $5k, vol ≥ $10k. Persisted to `tracked_wallets` (now 67 rows).
2. `scripts/_sweep-wallets.ts` (new, read-only) — per candidate: activity event mix (MERGE/SPLIT/REWARD ⇒ maker
   fingerprint), trades (clip, cadence, price), closed-positions realized stats via
   `src/lib/wallets/wallet-verification.ts` (`walletStatsFromClosed`/`verifyWalletStats` — realized PnL, NOT
   leaderboard ROI), `lb-api.polymarket.com` all-time profit+volume (margin on volume), `user-pnl-api` span +
   max drawdown. Raw output: `data/sweep-2026-06-10.json` (+ `…-shortlist.json` full pagination for finalists).
3. Shortlist deep-dive: full closed-positions pagination (endpoint caps at 50/page; offset works), trade-timestamp
   vs game-time inspection, `npm run classify:wallet -- --persist` repo typology on the top two.

**Survivorship honesty:** 101 distinct wallets appeared across the three leaderboards → 36 passed the sustained
filter and were examined in depth → **3 candidates (all conditional) + 1 watch**. The cohort is itself
survivorship-selected (leaderboards only show winners); the all-time-profit cross-check below is what catches the
mirages — 6 of 36 "sustained performers" are **all-time net losers** riding a hot recent window.

**Data caveats (skeptic + advocate):**
- `data-api /closed-positions` returns ≤50 rows/page regardless of `limit`; headline-table realized stats are the
  most recent ≤50 closed positions. Shortlist wallets were fully paginated.
- `data-api` closed-position `realizedPnl` sums exceed `lb-api /profit` all-time (different accounting — lb-api
  nets open-position mark and history outside the window). Where they disagree, the LOWER lb-api number is used
  for verification. Both must be positive.
- Python urllib's default UA is blocked by Polymarket edge (silent empties) — use a browser UA or `curl`/the
  repo's `poly` client. Data-api reads were NOT geo-blocked from this IP today; `POLYMARKET_PROXY_URL` +
  `proxy-routing.ts` remain available if that changes.
- Missing data ⇒ UNVERIFIED, not DEAD. DEAD is reserved for negative evidence (all-time realized loss).

## Headline finding

**The Polymarket taker leaderboard is dominated by LIVE-GAME sports traders, not pre-event forecasters.**
Every high-margin taker we opened (ethanaz, alwaysfade, joblessfinalboss, 0x418d51e1, surfandturf, Vatrer) enters
DURING games — NBA/MLB/Valorant — buying win-probability the market hasn't repriced from the live feed. Their edge
is a faster/better in-game read. That is the same structural problem as coinman2's maker edge: **naive copying
inherits the entries at post-move prices.** The two-gate check (mechanically copyable AND verified realized edge)
passes nobody cleanly this sweep; the three candidates below pass gate 2 outright and gate 1 only conditionally —
each needs a lag-aware copy-backtest (`npm run copy:backtest`, entry-impulse + shuffle) before any forward shadow.

## Candidates — strategy reverse-engineering

### 1. `0xf28e42d20e4826f2b10a24bc952001697947cab2` (ethanaz) — CANDIDATE (conditional)
**Verified realized:** +$2.46M across **716 closed positions / 638 distinct markets over 184 days** (full
pagination), win rate 88%, profit factor 9.66; lb-api all-time +$798k on $16.1M volume (5.0% margin); max DD
-$168k. Passes `verifyWalletStats` by a mile — this is the most verified taker edge found.
**Strategy:** in-game NBA (plus some MLB) live trading. Buys the side whose win-probability is rising during the
game — tape shows 15 buys in one SAS@NYK game, price ladder 0.61→0.86 as the game progressed; 68% of winning
positions entered below 0.65. Median clip $1.2k, ~11 trades/day, ~6.5 markets/day, holds to resolution (hours).
Portfolio ~$246k. Repo typology: "unclear/needs_more_data" (the in-game cadence confuses the buckets).
**Why conditional:** entries are DURING games — a copier fills after the move that motivated the entry. With PF
9.66 there is headroom for slippage, but that must be measured, not assumed. **Falsifier:** lag-aware
`copy:backtest` (60s/300s entry lag + shuffle control); if net edge at 300s lag ≤ 0, demote to
NOT-COPYABLE-TIMING.

### 2. `0xe5b70fd855af9258d9463992e4f1ed7987905ee3` (alwaysfade) — CANDIDATE (conditional)
**Verified realized:** +$1.16M across only **40 closed positions / 38 markets over 224 days**, win rate 93%,
profit factor 67; lb-api all-time +$809k on just $6.1M volume — **13.3% margin**, the fattest per-dollar edge in
the cohort. Max DD -$305k.
**Strategy:** highly selective live-NBA dip buyer (the name is literal — it fades in-game panic). Buys at
0.11-0.43 during games when a quality team falls behind (e.g., Spurs at 0.11-0.34 mid-game across the
SAS/NYK/OKC playoff run), holds to resolution. ~5 positions/month, $3-10k clips. Advocate case: entries this deep
leave enormous slippage headroom — paying 0.45 for what it bought at 0.35 still wins 93% of the time; and the
minutes-long dip windows (not seconds) make following plausible. Skeptic case: 40 bets is thin for a 93% win
rate (a couple of tail losses change the story), and the playoff-comeback regime may not persist.
**Falsifier:** forward shadow-track via `observe:wallet` — measure our achievable fill price N minutes after each
of its entries for ≥20 new positions; demote if realized follow-edge ≤ 0.

**No wallet passes both gates unconditionally.** Zero unconditional copy candidates from 101 wallets examined is
a finding, not a failure — it is consistent with the prior cohort's forward falsification and with coinman2:
on Polymarket, sustained taker profit at leaderboard scale comes overwhelmingly from live-event information speed,
which does not survive copy-lag by construction. The honest next step is to MEASURE the lag tolerance of the three
conditional candidates rather than assume it away in either direction.
